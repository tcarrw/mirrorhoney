How to identify the two halves

🍯 HONEY (Sabrina) — Sweet / Magnetic / Immediate

  • Feel: warm pull, flirt, charm, joy, “come closer.”
  • Body: head-nod, hum along, dance instinct; easy sing-along hooks.
  • Lyrics: sensory, taste/skin/heat, playfully direct, present-tense.
  • Production: glossy/pop-forward; bass + beat invite movement.
  • Aftertaste: you want to hit repeat; the hook sticks like syrup.

Shortcut: If the song feels like taste + touch and you move without thinking → it’s Honey.

🪞 MIRROR (Marina) — Reflection / Angle / Identity

  • Feel: thoughtful tilt, self-regard, critique, “look again.”
  • Mind: you parse lines; the meaning unfolds on the second pass.
  • Lyrics: persona, roles, masks, power, fame, culture, self-myth.
  • Production: prismatic/art-pop edges; arrangement carries the idea.
  • Afterimage: you leave with a frame or question rather than a hook.

Shortcut: If the song makes you see yourself or culture differently → it’s Mirror.

30-second test
  1. Play the chorus once.
  2. Ask: “Did my body move first (Honey) or did a thought click first (Mirror)?”
  3. If both, which feeling is primary? That’s your tag.
Edge cases & tie-breakers
  • Sweet lyrics, reflective bridge → still Honey if the pull leads.
  • Danceable production, heavy critique → still Mirror if the angle leads.
  • Unsure? Circle the lyric that hit hardest. If it’s touch/taste → Honey. If it’s role/idea → Mirror.
